## Key Takeaways
- Post-rollout AI drift is a primary quality degradation risk requiring proactive governance
- Trust protection requires explicit ownership structures, not emergent monitoring
- Stable quality post-launch demands continuous calibration, not one-time validation

## Guardrails & Anti-patterns
- **Anti-pattern:** Treating AI rollout as a completion event rather than a launch state
- **Anti-pattern:** Assuming initial validation performance persists without active guardrails
- **Anti-pattern:** Distributing AI accountability across teams without clear ownership

## Implementation Steps
- Establish explicit AI ownership (single accountable owner for quality, performance, escalation handling)
- Define drift detection triggers and thresholds before rollout
- Build continuous feedback loops from production use back to improvement cycles
- Implement post-launch calibration checkpoints at defined intervals (frequency not specified in preview)

- **"From AI Use to AI Ownership"** — transition model moving from experimental deployment to operationalized, governed AI systems
