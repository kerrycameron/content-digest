# AI in Support, Part 3: Teaching Agents To Prompt Without Turning Them Into Prompt Engineers

**Source:** https://1fernandoduarte.substack.com/p/ai-in-support-part-3-teaching-agents

**Date:** December 1, 2025

**Category:** 1. AI in Customer Support

**Recency Score:** 0-6 months (full weight)

## Key Takeaways
- Training support staff on AI tooling should focus on practical habits, not prompt engineering expertise
- Avoid creating a "second job" of mastering prompting techniques for tier-1/tier-2 support agents
- Simple, repeatable workflows reduce cognitive load and improve consistency

## Guardrails & Anti-patterns
- **Anti-pattern:** Requiring support agents to become prompt engineers or AI experts as a prerequisite to using AI tools
- **Guard:** Design AI workflows with pre-configured prompts and templates that agents activate rather than construct
- **Guard:** Establish clear boundaries on what agents customize (parameters only) vs. what stays templated (prompt structure)

## Implementation Steps
- Abstract prompt complexity away from agents through UI/UX design (buttons, dropdowns, pre-loaded templates)
- Create habit-based training focused on "when to use this tool" and "what output to expect"
- Layer advanced prompting as optional upskilling, not baseline requirement
